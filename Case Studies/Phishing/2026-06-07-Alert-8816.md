## 🔍 2. Analysis & Escalation

> **Classification Verdict: TRUE POSITIVE**  
> The firewall successfully blocked an outbound connection to a blacklisted malicious domain that utilized a `bit.ly` link shortener to obfuscate its true destination.

* **Escalation Required?** ❌ **No.**
* **Justification:** The threat was successfully mitigated at the network perimeter. A subsequent forensic scan of the endpoint (`10.20.2.17`) logs showed 0 malicious events, confirming no host-level compromise occurred.

---

## 🎯 3. Attack Indicators

* **Obfuscation:** Use of a `bit.ly` commercial link shortener to hide the real destination domain from basic inspection.
* **Unauthorized Outbound Traffic:** Attempted communication with a known, blacklisted external IP address.
* **Protocol Anomaly:** Outbound web request sent over the insecure, unencrypted Port 80 (HTTP) rather than standard HTTPS.

---

## 🛡️ 4. Remediation Actions

1. **Forensics:** Scan endpoint `10.20.2.17` to verify the absence of active malware, persistence mechanisms, or suspicious child processes.
2. **Train:** Enroll the user in targeted phishing awareness training, specifically highlighting the dangers of clicking shortened or obfuscated links.
3. **Monitor:** Continue monitoring the endpoint's network logs for any repeated anomalous outbound connection attempts.
